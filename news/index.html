Founder Discusses Game-Based History Learning On A Podcast

The founder of the project recently took part in an interview focused on the use of real-time strategy (RTS) games as educational tools for teaching history to children. During the interview, the founder explained how strategy games allow students to actively engage with historical concepts rather than simply memorizing dates and facts. Topics discussed included how managing resources, making strategic decisions, and responding to in-game events can help students better understand cause-and-effect relationships in historical contexts. The founder also highlighted how RTS gameplay encourages critical thinking, problem-solving, and collaboration, all while reinforcing key historical themes. The interview emphasized the project's goal of making history more interactive and accessible for younger audiences by combining curriculum-based learning with engaging gameplay experiences.

New Office Opens

We have officially opened a new educational development office to support the continued growth of the project. The office will serve as a collaborative space for designing and testing RTS-based history learning experiences aimed at younger students. This new location will be used for curriculum research, lesson planning, and the development of historically accurate game scenarios. It will also allow closer collaboration between game designers and educators to ensure that gameplay mechanics align with educational goals. The opening of the office marks an important step in expanding the project's ability to create engaging, interactive tools that help students explore history through strategy-based learning.
